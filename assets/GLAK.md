# (PhilID / National ID)

### **Prerequisites**

Before heading to a registration center, you only need to prepare your supporting documents. The registration process itself is entirely **FREE** of charge.

* **Bring ONE (1) Original Primary Document:**  
  * PSA-issued Certificate of Live Birth (plus one government-issued ID with a photo and signature).  
  * Valid Philippine Passport or ePassport.  
  * UMID Card (issued by SSS or GSIS).  
  * Driver’s License or Student Permit (issued by LTO).  
* **Alternative (If you do not have a Primary Document):** Bring original copies of secondary documents that show your full name, front-facing photograph, signature/thumbmark, permanent address, and date of birth (e.g., PRC ID, Senior Citizen ID, Voter's ID, NBI Clearance, School ID, or Barangay ID/Clearance).  
* *Note: Ensure you bring the physical, original documents, as photocopies will not be accepted for primary data entry.*

### **The Process**

**1.Walk In to a Registration Center:**  
Locate the nearest PhilSys Registration Center (found in PSA Regional/Provincial Offices, major shopping malls, or local government units). No prior booking or appointment slip is needed—simply walk in and queue.

**2.Document Verification:**  
Present your original supporting documents to the Registration Kit Operator (RKO) at the counter. The officer will verify your identity and manually encode your demographic data (full name, birthdate, address, blood type, etc.) into the system. You will be asked to review the screen to confirm your details are spelled correctly.

**3.Biometric Data Collection:**  
Once your data is confirmed, the RKO will capture your biometrics. This involves scanning all ten fingerprints, an iris scan of both eyes, and taking a front-facing photograph.

**4.Collect Your Transaction Slip:**Crucial for digital access.  
After completing your biometrics, the operator will hand you a transaction slip containing your **Transaction Reference Number (TRN)**. Keep this slip safe; it is your permanent tracking number and your key to accessing your digital ID formats while waiting for the physical card.

**5.Access Your Digital ID or ePhilID:**  
While waiting for the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) to print your physical card, you can immediately use your National ID in two ways:

* **Digital National ID:** Download the official **eGovPH Super App** on your smartphone. Register an account, navigate to the Digital National ID section, and input your TRN and demographic details to generate your digital ID on your phone.  
* **Printed ePhilID:** You can request a printed version of your ePhilID (a paper format with a QR code) directly at the registration center or at designated PSA claiming centers.

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Registration Time:** The entire walk-in process at the center usually takes only **30 to 60 minutes**, depending on the queue.  
* **Digital National ID / ePhilID:** Can be accessed via the eGovPH app or printed at claiming centers almost **immediately** or within a few days after receiving your TRN. Both are legally valid as proof of identity for all government and private transactions.  
* **Physical PhilID Card:** The physical polycarbonate card is printed by the BSP and delivered door-to-door via PHLPost. Delivery timelines vary wildly (ranging from a few months to over a year), which is why the PSA strongly advises using the eGovPH app or printed ePhilID in the meantime.

# Philippine Passport

### **Prerequisites**

Before securing an appointment, ensure you have the following requirements on hand. Bring the original documents and at least one clear photocopy of each to your appointment:

* **Valid Government-Issued ID:** One (1) primary ID (e.g., PhilSys National ID, ePhilID, UMID, Driver’s License, Postal ID, PRC ID).  
* **PSA Birth Certificate:** An original copy of your Certificate of Live Birth issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) on security paper. If the entries are blurred or unreadable, you must also secure a transcribed certified true copy from your Local Civil Registrar.  
* **PSA Marriage Certificate:** Only required for married women who are applying to use their husband’s surname.  
* **A Confirmed Online Appointment:** An appointment slot secured through the official DFA portal.  
* **Passport Fees:**  
  * **Regular Processing:** ₱950.00  
  * **Expedited Processing:** ₱1,200.00  
  * *(Note: A convenience fee of around ₱50 is usually added by authorized payment centers).*

### **The Process**

**1.Book an Online Appointment:**  
Go to the official DFA Passport Appointment Portal (**passport.gov.ph**) and choose between an individual or group appointment. Select your preferred DFA Consular Office, date, and time. Fill out the online application form completely and accurately.

**2.Pay the Processing Fee:**Must be completed within 24 hours.  
At the end of the booking process, choose your preferred processing type (Regular or Expedited). You will generate a reference number. Use this number to pay the exact fee at any authorized DFA payment center (e.g., 7-Eleven, Bayad Center, GCash, PayMaya, or over-the-counter banks) within 24 hours to secure your slot.

**3.Print Your Application Packet:**  
Once your payment is posted, the DFA will email you a confirmed appointment packet. Print all the documents contained in the PDF on **A4-size paper**: the completed application form with the barcode, your appointment checklist, and your e-Receipt.

**4.Attend Your DFA Appointment:**Personal appearance is strictly required.  
Go to your chosen DFA Consular Office on the exact date and time of your appointment. Bring your printed application packet and all your original documents with photocopies. Wear appropriate attire (smart casual; no sleeveless tops or slippers).

**5.Document Verification and Biometrics:**  
Submit your documents to the evaluating officer. Once cleared, proceed to the data capture area. You will be asked to remove your eyeglasses, contact lenses, and heavy makeup for your digital photograph, and you will provide your digital signature and fingerprints.

**6.Claim Your Passport:**  
Wait for the release of your passport. You can choose to pick it up personally at the DFA office on the scheduled release date or opt for a courier delivery service (for an additional fee) during your application.

### **Estimated Timelines**

The processing time begins on the day you successfully complete your biometrics and data capture at the DFA office:

| Location | Regular Processing | Expedited Processing |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Metro Manila Offices** | \~10 to 12 working days | \~5 to 6 working days |
| **Outside Metro Manila (Provincial)** | \~12 working days | \~7 working days |

# Drivers License

**Important Note:** The Land Transportation Office (LTO) strictly enforces a two-tier system. You *cannot* directly apply for a driver's license. You must first secure a **Student-Driver's Permit (SP)** and hold it for at least one (1) month before you are legally allowed to apply for a Non-Professional Driver's License.

### **Prerequisites**

Before heading to the LTO to convert your Student Permit to a Driver's License, you must prepare the following:

* **Active LTMS Portal Account:** You must be registered on the Land Transportation Management System (LTMS) portal (portal.lto.gov.ph). Your LTO Client Number acts as your lifelong profile.  
* **Valid Student Permit:** Must be valid and issued at least 31 days prior to your application date.  
* **Practical Driving Course (PDC) Certificate:** A certificate of completion from an LTO-accredited driving school or LTO Driver's Education Center. (The driving school will electronically transmit this to your LTMS account, but bring the physical copy).  
* **Medical Certificate:** Issued by an LTO-accredited medical clinic within the last 60 days. (The clinic will also electronically transmit this to your LTMS profile).  
* **Application for Permits and License (APL) Form:** Duly accomplished form (available at the LTO or downloadable online).  
* **Tax Identification Number (TIN):** Mandatory if you are employed.  
* **For 17-year-old applicants:** A notarized consent letter from a parent or legal guardian, along with a photocopy of the parent's valid government ID.  
* **Estimated Fees:**  
  * **LTO Fees:** Application Fee (₱100) \+ License Fee (₱585) \= **₱685.00**  
  * *Other variable costs:* Medical Exam (₱300-₱500) and Private PDC Course (₱2,500-₱6,000 depending on the vehicle type; free if taken at an LTO Driver's Education Center, subject to slot availability).

### **The Process**

**1.Create and Check Your LTMS Account:**  
Ensure your LTMS Portal account is active. Check your dashboard to verify that your chosen driving school and medical clinic have successfully transmitted your PDC Certificate and Medical Certificate to the LTO system.

**2.Visit the LTO Branch:**Walk-ins are accepted, but online appointments save time.  
Proceed to any LTO District Office or Licensing Center. Go to the Customer Service or Evaluation window and submit your original Student Permit, Medical Certificate, PDC Certificate, and accomplished APL form.

**3.Pay the Application Fee:**  
Once your documents are evaluated and verified in the LTMS system, your name will be called at the cashier. Pay the non-refundable **₱100.00** Application Fee and secure the Official Receipt (OR).

**4.Take the Automated Theoretical Exam:**You must score at least 48 out of 60 to pass.  
Proceed to the examination room. You will take a computerized multiple-choice exam covering traffic laws, road signs, and safe driving practices. If you fail, you cannot proceed to the next step and must return another day to retake the exam.

**5.Take the Practical Driving Test:**  
If you pass the theoretical exam, you will be escorted to the driving track. You must demonstrate your ability to safely operate a vehicle (in the category you are applying for, e.g., manual or automatic motorcycle/car) in front of an LTO examiner. You can rent a vehicle on-site if you did not bring one.

**6.Biometrics and Payment:**  
After passing the practical exam, return to the LTO building. Wait for your name to be called for biometrics capture (photograph, digital signature, and fingerprints). Afterward, proceed to the cashier to pay the **₱585.00** License Fee.

**7.Claim Your Driver's License:**  
Wait at the releasing area. You will be asked to sign the release logbook and receive your physical Driver's License card (or an official paper receipt serving as your temporary license if the branch is out of plastic cards) along with the Official Receipt.

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time at the LTO:** The entire process—from document evaluation to examinations and card releasing—typically takes **3 to 5 hours** depending on the queue length and system stability at the specific branch. It is highly recommended to arrive before 8:00 AM.  
* **License Validity:** A newly issued Non-Professional Driver's License is valid for **five (5) years**. If you maintain a clean driving record (no traffic violations) during those five years, you will be eligible for a ten-year (10) license validity upon your first renewal.

# Unified Multi-Purpose ID (UMID)

You might hit a wall if you try to apply for a standard UMID card today. **The generic Unified Multi-Purpose ID (UMID) has been officially phased out.**

As of late 2025, both the SSS and GSIS permanently stopped producing new generic UMID cards to align with the government's push for the PhilSys National ID.

If you are a **new applicant**, you can no longer get a UMID. You must instead apply for its direct replacement depending on your sector: the **MySSS Card** (for private employees) or the **GSIS Digital ID & ATM** (for government employees), both of which we covered earlier.

However, if you **already have an old physical UMID card**, the only active UMID process left in 2026 is upgrading your old ID into a **UMID ATM Pay Card** (in partnership with UnionBank). If this applies to you, here is the guide for the upgrade.

### **Prerequisites**

Before starting the upgrade process, ensure you have the following:

* **Existing UMID:** You must already possess a physical SSS-issued UMID card.  
* **Active My.SSS Account:** Your online portal account must be active.  
* **Updated Records:** Your contact information (mobile number and email) must be completely up to date in the SSS database to receive the One-Time Passwords (OTPs).  
* **Smartphone:** You need a smartphone to download the partner bank's mobile app.  
* **Fees:** Upgrading to the UMID ATM Pay Card is **FREE**.

### **The Process**

**1.Provide Consent via My.SSS:**  
Log in to your My.SSS account on a browser. A pop-up notification regarding the UMID ATM Pay Card Upgrade should appear (you can also find it under the E-Services menu). Click **Provide Consent** to authorize the SSS to share your demographic data and UMID records with UnionBank.

**2.Download the UnionBank Online App:**  
Install the UnionBank Mobile App from the App Store or Google Play Store on your smartphone. Open the app and select the option to open a new account.

**3.Select the Government Card Option:**Required for data syncing.  
Choose **Government Cards with Savings Account**, then select the SSS UMID Pay Card. You will be prompted to enter your SSS details. Make sure the information you type matches exactly what is on your My.SSS profile.

**4.Complete the eKYC Verification:**  
Wait for the OTP sent to your registered mobile number. You will then fill out the remaining financial details, perform a live selfie scan for security, and provide three digital signatures on your screen.

**5.Card Delivery and Activation:**  
Choose your preferred delivery address. Once the physical UnionBank UMID ATM Pay Card arrives at your doorstep, log back into the UnionBank app to activate the card and set your 6-digit ATM PIN. This account will automatically become your default disbursement account for future SSS benefits.

### **Estimated Timelines**

Once your account is successfully opened via the UnionBank app, the card production and delivery will follow this schedule:

* **Metro Manila:** Within 15 banking days  
* **Outside Metro Manila (Provincial):** Within 20 banking days

# Postal ID

### **4r Prerequisites**

Before heading to a PHLPost branch, prepare the following requirements. Bring the original copies and at least one (1) photocopy of each:

* **Application Form:** Two (2) copies of the duly accomplished Postal ID (PID) Application Form. You can download and print this from the PHLPost website or get it for free at any post office.  
* **Proof of Identity (Primary):** Bring any ONE (1) of the following:  
  * PSA-issued Birth Certificate  
  * PhilSys National ID / ePhilID  
  * GSIS / SSS UMID Card  
  * Driver’s License  
  * Valid Passport  
  * *(If you don't have a primary ID, you must bring TWO (2) secondary IDs, at least one bearing your signature and photo, such as a PhilHealth ID, TIN Card, or Company/School ID).*  
* **Proof of Address:** Bring any ONE (1) of the following:  
  * Barangay Certificate of Residency issued within three months prior to application  
  * Utility bill (electric, water, internet) under your name  
  * Bank statement or credit card billing  
* **Fees:**  
  * **Regular Processing:** ₱550.00 (inclusive of tax and delivery fee)  
  * **Rush Processing:** ₱650.00

### **The Process**

**1.Accomplish the Application Form:**  
Fill out the two copies of the PID Application Form. Ensure all information is accurate and matches your supporting documents. Leave no blanks; write "N/A" if a field does not apply to you.

**2.Submit Documents and Pay the Fee:**  
Proceed to the nearest PHLPost branch or designated ID capture station. Submit your forms and original documents for verification. Once cleared, pay the application fee (Regular or Rush) at the cashier and keep your Official Receipt.

**3.Biometrics Data Capture:**  
After payment, you will be directed to the data capture booth. An operator will take your digital photograph, record your fingerprints using a biometric scanner, and capture your digital signature.

**4.Wait for Delivery:**  
Unlike other IDs that you have to pick up, the Regular Postal ID will be delivered directly to your registered mailing address via PHLPost. (If you opted for the Rush Processing at select major branches like the Manila Central Post Office, you will be instructed to wait in the releasing area).

### **Estimated Timelines**

The processing and delivery times depend on your chosen application type and location:

* **Regular Processing:**  
  * **Metro Manila:** Delivered within **10 to 15 working days**.  
  * **Major Cities & Provinces:** Delivered within **20 to 30 working days**.  
  * **Island Municipalities:** Up to **4 weeks**.  
* **Rush Processing (Available at select major hubs like the Manila Central Post Office):**  
  * **Same-Day Release:** If you complete your biometrics data capture between 8:00 AM and 11:00 AM, the ID is released on the same day. Applications processed in the afternoon are typically released the next working day.

*Note: The Improved Postal ID is valid for three (3) years for Filipinos.*

# Voter's ID / Voter's Certification

 It is important to know that the Commission on Elections (COMELEC) has **permanently stopped printing and issuing the physical Voter's ID cards** to pave the way for the PhilSys National ID.

As a direct replacement, COMELEC issues the **Voter's Certification**. This printed document bears the official COMELEC dry seal and serves as an official, widely accepted proof of voter registration for passport applications, government transactions, and other legal purposes.

### **Prerequisites**

Before securing your certification, prepare the following:

* **Active Registration Status:** You must be an active registered voter. If your record was deactivated (usually for failing to vote in two consecutive regular elections), you cannot get a certification until you reactivate your status during a voter registration period.  
* **Valid Identification:** Bring the original and at least one (1) photocopy of a valid government-issued ID (e.g., PhilSys National ID, Passport, Driver's License, Postal ID, SSS/GSIS ID).  
* **Fees:**  
  * The standard certification fee is **₱75.00**.  
  * *Exemptions:* The certificate is entirely **FREE** for Senior Citizens, Persons with Disabilities (PWDs), Members of Indigenous Peoples (IPs), and Indigent citizens (must present a Certificate of Indigency from the barangay or DSWD).  
* **If applying via representative:** A formal authorization letter signed by the registered voter, along with the original and photocopies of valid IDs from both the voter and the representative.

### **The Process**

**1.Visit a COMELEC Office:**  
Go to the local Office of the Election Officer (OEO) in the city or municipality where you are registered. If you are currently in Metro Manila but registered in a distant province, you can visit the National Central File Division at the COMELEC Main Office in Intramuros, Manila.

**2.Verify Your Registration Record:**  
Approach the frontline desk or the Public Assistance and Information Desk (PAID) and present your valid ID. The officer will search the Voter Registration System (VRS) database to verify your active status and biometric records.

**3.Pay the Certification Fee:**  
Once your record is verified, you will be directed to the designated Cashier (or the City/Municipal Treasurer's Office, depending on your local government's setup). Pay the ₱75.00 fee and secure your Official Receipt (OR). If you are exempted, present your proof of exemption instead.

**4.Claim Your Certification:**  
Return to the COMELEC officer and present your OR. They will print your Voter's Certification, stamp it with the official COMELEC dry seal, and have it signed by the Election Officer. Double-check that your name and precinct details are spelled correctly before leaving.

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time:** Whether you apply at your local OEO or the COMELEC Main Office, the Voter's Certification is processed and released **on the same day** (usually within 15 to 30 minutes for local offices, though the Main Office may take a bit longer depending on the queue).  
* **Validity:** The Voter's Certification itself does not have a formal expiration date. However, most requesting agencies (such as the DFA for passport applications) require the certification to have been issued within the last **six (6) months** to ensure your registration status is current.

## **Medical Certificate (for PWD ID)**

**Prerequisites**

Before visiting a specialist to obtain a Medical Certificate, prepare the following:

* **Patient Identification**: A valid government-issued ID or any ID for patient identification.  
* **Existing Medical Records**: Bring any previously issued diagnostic results, imaging, laboratory tests, or prescriptions relevant to your disability. Examples include an audiogram for hearing impairment, an ophthalmology report for visual impairment, a psychiatric evaluation for psychosocial disability, or oncology/nephrology records for chronic illness.  
* **Referral Letter**: Some specialist clinics or hospital departments require a referral from a general practitioner before accepting a consultation. Check with the clinic in advance.  
* **PhilHealth ID or Member Data Record (MDR)**: Bring your PhilHealth documents if you intend to use your coverage to offset consultation fees.

**Fees**: Consultation fees vary. Government or public hospitals typically charge ₱100 to ₱300, and some waive the fee entirely for indigent patients. Private specialist clinics range from ₱500 to ₱2,500 or more. A separate medical certificate fee of approximately ₱200 to ₱500 may also be charged at private clinics, while this is usually minimal or included at public hospitals.

---

**The Process**

1. Identify the correct specialist for your disability type. The certificate must come from a licensed doctor whose specialization is relevant to your condition — an ophthalmologist for visual impairment, an ENT specialist or audiologist for hearing impairment, a psychiatrist for psychosocial or intellectual disability, an orthopedic surgeon or physiatrist for physical/orthopedic impairment, and an oncologist, nephrologist, or relevant internist for chronic illnesses such as cancer or kidney failure.  
2. Book an appointment. Schedule a consultation at a government hospital (e.g., Philippine General Hospital, a regional or provincial hospital, or your Rural Health Unit) or a private specialist clinic. Inform the clinic at the time of booking that you need a Medical Certificate specifically for PWD ID application purposes so they can prepare accordingly.  
3. Attend the consultation and bring all relevant medical records. Present your existing diagnostic results, imaging, and records to the doctor. The specialist will assess your condition and the nature of your disability.  
4. Request the Medical Certificate for PWD ID. Confirm with the doctor that the certificate must include: your full name and date of birth, the diagnosis and type of disability, a statement on whether the condition is permanent or temporary, and the doctor's full name, PRC license number, and signature on clinic or hospital letterhead with an official stamp.  
5. Pay the consultation and medical certificate fees at the billing or cashier window before claiming the document.  
6. Claim and review the Medical Certificate. Before leaving, verify that the PRC license number is clearly visible, the diagnosis explicitly mentions your disability, and all personal details are accurate and complete.

---

**Estimated Timelines**

**Appointment Wait Time**: At government hospitals, securing a specialist appointment may take anywhere from 1 day to 2 weeks depending on queue volume and doctor availability. Private clinics can typically accommodate patients within the same day to 3 days.

**Consultation Duration:** The consultation itself usually takes 15 to 45 minutes.

**Certificate Release**: Many private clinics release the certificate on the same day after consultation. Some clinics, particularly for detailed assessments (e.g., psychiatric or developmental evaluations), may take 1 to 3 working days to prepare the document.

**Total Estimated Time**: At a private clinic, the entire process from booking to receiving the certificate typically takes 1 to 2 days. At a government hospital, account for up to 2 to 3 weeks if specialist queues are long.

Tip: Some LGUs have free medical assessment programs specifically for PWD ID applicants. Contact your local PDAO before scheduling a private consultation to check if this option is available in your area.

**Income Tax Return (ITR)**

Prerequisites

For Employed Individuals (BIR Form 2316 or 1700):

Before obtaining your ITR, prepare the following:

BIR Form 2316: Your employer is legally required to issue you a BIR Form 2316 (Certificate of Compensation Payment and Tax Withheld) at the end of every calendar year. This is the most commonly accepted substitute for a formal ITR filing for purely compensation-income earners. Request this directly from your employer's HR or payroll department — no BIR visit is needed.

For Self-Employed, Mixed-Income, or Business Owners (BIR Form 1701 or 1701A):

* BIR Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN): You must be registered with the BIR and have an active TIN.  
* Books of accounts or financial records for the taxable year (income and expense records).  
* BIR Form 1701 or 1701A — the Annual Income Tax Return form, available for free at any BIR Revenue District Office (RDO) or downloadable from the BIR website (bir.gov.ph).  
* Payment for any taxes due: If you have a tax liability after computing your return, payment is made via BIR-accredited banks or via GCash/Maya through the eBIRForms or eFPS system.  
* Filing fee: No fee for filing. Payment is only required if taxes are due based on your computation.

---

The Process

For Employed Individuals

1. Request BIR Form 2316 from your employer's HR or payroll department. You are entitled to receive this by January 31 of the following year. If it has not been provided, formally request it in writing.  
2. Use the Form 2316 as your ITR substitute. For purely compensation-income earners whose employer withholds the correct tax, the signed Form 2316 is legally accepted in place of a filed ITR for most government purposes, including Solo Parent ID applications.

---

For Self-Employed or Mixed-Income Earners

1. Prepare your Annual Income Tax Return using BIR Form 1701 or 1701A. Compute your total gross income, deductions, and tax due for the calendar year. You may use the BIR's free eBIRForms software (downloadable from bir.gov.ph) to fill out and validate the form digitally.  
2. File your ITR at your Revenue District Office (RDO) or online via eFPS. Self-employed individuals registered with eBIRForms must submit their return online. Over-the-counter filing is available at your registered RDO for those not enrolled in eFPS.  
3. Pay any tax due at an Authorized Agent Bank (AAB) near your RDO, or via GCash, Maya, or Landbank's e-payment portal if filing electronically. If no tax is due (zero tax or refund), payment is not required.  
4. Receive your stamped or system-validated ITR copy. For over-the-counter filing, the RDO stamps your copy as received. For electronic filing, download and save the Filing Reference Number (FRN) confirmation as your proof of filing.

---

Estimated Timelines

BIR Form 2316 (employed): Issued by your employer by January 31 each year. If requesting mid-year, HR typically releases it within 1 to 5 working days.

ITR filing (self-employed, over-the-counter): The filing itself takes 30 minutes to 1.5 hours at the RDO, depending on queue length. Preparation of financial records may take several days beforehand.

Deadline for Annual ITR filing: April 15 of every year for the preceding taxable year.

Note: If you have never filed an ITR and are not required to (i.e., purely employed with Form 2316), the Form 2316 alone is sufficient. You do not need to separately file an ITR.

## **Certificate of Finality *(For Annulled Solo Parents)***

Prerequisites

Before requesting a Certificate of Finality, understand that this document is issued by the Regional Trial Court (RTC) that handled your annulment case, confirming that the decision has become final and executory (meaning no appeal was filed within the prescribed period). Prepare the following:

Case details: The RTC branch and court location where your annulment was decided, your case number, and the date of the court's decision.

Valid ID: One (1) valid government-issued ID for identity verification at the clerk of court's office.

Authorization (if requesting via representative): A notarized Special Power of Attorney (SPA) authorizing the representative to request court documents on your behalf, plus a photocopy of the representative's valid ID.

Certified True Copy fee: Typically ₱500 to ₱1,500 depending on the number of pages and the RTC's schedule of fees. Fees are paid directly to the Office of the Clerk of Court.

Note: If your annulment was granted through a church declaration of nullity (Canon Law) rather than a civil court, you must obtain a certified copy of the church decree from the National Tribunal of Appeals or the diocesan tribunal that issued it. A church declaration alone, however, does not dissolve the civil marriage — a separate civil court proceeding is required for civil effects.

---

The Process

1. Locate the RTC branch that handled your case. Contact or visit the Regional Trial Court where your annulment petition was filed and decided. Bring your case number if available. If you do not recall the specific branch, the Office of the Clerk of Court at the RTC Complex of your city or province can help locate the record.  
2. Go to the Office of the Clerk of Court. Proceed to the civil or family court section and inform the clerk that you are requesting a Certificate of Finality and a Certified True Copy of the Decision for your annulment case.  
3. Submit a written request or fill out the court's request form. Some RTC branches require a formal written request addressed to the Presiding Judge or the Clerk of Court. State your full name, case number, nature of the request, and purpose (Solo Parent ID application).  
4. Await verification of the records. The clerk will locate your case records and verify whether the decision has indeed become final and executory — meaning the 15-day appeal period (or the period set in the decision) has lapsed with no appeal filed by either party.  
5. Pay the certification fee at the Office of the Clerk of Court cashier. Keep your official receipt as this is required before the documents are released.  
6. Receive the Certificate of Finality and Certified True Copy of the Decision. The Certificate of Finality is signed by the Branch Clerk of Court and confirms the date on which the decision became final. This document, together with the certified copy of the decision, constitutes your legal proof of annulment for Solo Parent ID purposes.  
7. Have the documents authenticated if required. Some LGU offices may require the court documents to carry a red-ribbon certification or Apostille for authenticity. Confirm with your local MSWDO or PDAO whether this additional step is necessary.

Estimated Timelines

Record retrieval and verification by the court: 1 to 5 working days for active or recent cases. Older cases stored in archives may take 1 to 3 weeks to retrieve.

Preparation and signing of Certificate of Finality: 3 to 7 working days after verification, depending on the court's workload and the judge or clerk's schedule.

Total estimated time: 1 to 4 weeks from the date of request, depending on the RTC branch and record availability.

Important Note: If your annulment decision was issued very recently and the appeal period has not yet lapsed, the court cannot issue a Certificate of Finality until the decision becomes final. The standard appeal period is 15 days from receipt of the decision by both parties.

**Certificate of Detention *(For Solo Parents Whose Spouse is Detained)***

Prerequisites

Before requesting a Certificate of Detention, prepare the following:

Full name and details of the detained spouse: Complete legal name, date of birth, and if known, the case number, criminal charge, and the name of the detention facility where the spouse is currently held.

Proof of your relationship to the detained person: A PSA Marriage Certificate confirming you are the legal spouse.

Valid ID: One (1) valid government-issued ID for identity verification at the detention facility's administrative office.

Authorization (if requesting via representative): A signed authorization letter and a photocopy of your valid ID if someone else is requesting on your behalf.

Fees: Typically free. Some facilities may charge a minimal certification or documentation fee of ₱50 to ₱200. No formal filing fee is required.

---

The Process

1. Identify the correct detention facility. Determine which facility currently holds your spouse — this may be the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP)-managed city or municipal jail, a provincial jail under the Bureau of Corrections (BuCor), or the New Bilibid Prison or similar national penitentiary for convicted prisoners serving longer sentences.  
2. Go to the administrative office of the detention facility. Proceed to the Records Section or Administrative Office. Do not go through the visitation area — go directly to the office handling inmate records and certifications.  
3. Submit a written request for a Certificate of Detention. Inform the records officer that you need an official certification confirming the name of the inmate, the date of commitment or detention, and the expected duration or current status of detention. State the purpose clearly — Solo Parent ID application — as this helps the officer include the relevant details.  
4. Present your valid ID and proof of relationship. Submit your government-issued ID and your PSA Marriage Certificate for verification. The officer will confirm the inmate's identity against their records.  
5. Wait for the certificate to be prepared and signed. The document must be signed by the Officer-in-Charge or the Jail Warden (for BJMP facilities) or the facility superintendent (for BuCor). It must state the inmate's full name, the offense or case, and that detention has been ongoing for more than one (1) year — this duration requirement is critical for Solo Parent ID qualification.  
6. Claim the Certificate of Detention and verify that the duration of detention is explicitly stated, the official's signature and designation are present, and the facility's official seal is affixed.

---

Estimated Timelines

Notarial fee: Typically ranges from ₱200 to ₱500 depending on the notary public or law office. Government employees may seek free notarization through their agency's legal officer or at the Public Attorney's Office (PAO) located at most city or municipal halls.

Fees: No newspaper publication is required for a lost government ID. The notarial fee is the only cost involved in obtaining this document.

---

The Process

1. Go to a notary public or law office. Commissioned notaries are commonly found at law offices near courthouses, inside malls, or at city and municipal hall complexes. Alternatively, check if your agency's Legal Division can assist with notarization for employees.  
2. Request an Affidavit of Loss specifically for a GSIS eCard. Inform the notary of the exact document lost, your GSIS Policy or BP Number, and the purpose — GSIS eCard replacement. This helps the notary use the correct template and include the details GSIS requires.  
3. Provide the complete details of the lost card. Tell the notary your full legal name, GSIS Policy Number or BP Number, the government agency you are employed with, and the full circumstances of the loss — when it was last in your possession, where and how it was lost, and that it has not been recovered.  
4. Review the drafted affidavit carefully. Before signing, confirm that your name is spelled exactly as it appears in your GSIS records, your Policy or BP Number is correctly stated, the account of loss is truthful and accurate, and the document is properly addressed.  
5. Sign the affidavit in the physical presence of the notary. You must sign in front of the notary — documents signed in advance cannot be legally notarized. Present your valid ID for the notary's records book.  
6. Pay the notarial fee and receive the notarized Affidavit of Loss bearing the notary's signature, official seal, and complete notarial details — document number, page, book, and series year.  
7. Make at least one photocopy of the notarized affidavit before submitting the original to GSIS. Keep one copy for your personal records.

---

Estimated Timelines

Affidavit drafting by the notary: 15 to 30 minutes using a standard template.

Notarization and release: Same day. The entire visit typically takes 30 minutes to 1 hour from arrival to claiming the document.

Total estimated time: The Affidavit of Loss can be completed within a single half-day visit to a notary public.

Important Note: GSIS requires the affidavit to be notarized — a plain written statement or an unnotarized sworn declaration is not accepted. Ensure the document bears the notary's dry seal, signature, complete notarial details, and the official documentary stamp tax before submitting it to the GSIS branch. Some GSIS offices may also have their own Affidavit of Loss template — confirm with your branch whether they require their specific form or if a standard notarized affidavit is acceptable.

**2.Secure Medical Clearance:**  
If you have a non-apparent disability, ensure your medical certificate from a specialist is recent. If you have an apparent disability but no medical certificate, you may be required to pass by the City/Municipal Health Office first for a quick assessment and to secure a local Certificate of Disability.

Once approved and signed by the Mayor or PDAO Head, your PWD ID will be released. Along with the ID card, you should also receive a **Medicine Booklet** and a **Grocery Booklet**, which are required when claiming your mandated 20% discount and 5% special discounts, respectively.

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time:** Depending on the LGU, walk-in applications are usually processed and released within **1 to 3 working days**. In LGUs with highly modernized PDAO facilities, the ID can be printed and issued **on the same day**.  
* **Validity:** The PWD ID is valid for **five (5) years** from the date of issuance and must be renewed following the same procedure (though a new medical certificate is generally only required if the disability was classified as temporary or progressive).

# Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) ID

Unlike standard government IDs (such as a Passport, PhilHealth, or Postal ID), the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) ID is strictly issued *only* to the head or grantee of a household that the government has officially selected to be part of the conditional cash transfer program.

As of recent updates following the CBMS Law, households are identified as "poor" or "near-poor" through the **Community-Based Monitoring System (CBMS)** data. If your family is identified by the system and meets the criteria (having pregnant women or children aged 0–18), the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) will proactively contact your local government unit (LGU) to schedule your registration.

If you have been selected or notified by your LGU for 4Ps inclusion, here is the guide to completing your registration and securing your 4Ps ID.

### **Prerequisites**

Before attending the scheduled DSWD Community Assembly or Registration One-Stop Shop, the household grantee must prepare the following:

* **Official Notification:** You must be on the official master list of eligible households provided by the DSWD/CBMS to your barangay.  
* **PhilSys National ID:** Required for identity authentication during registration. The DSWD strongly mandates the use of the National ID to streamline eligibility checks and digital payments.  
* **Family Documents (Originals and Photocopies):**  
  * PSA Birth Certificates of all household members (especially children aged 0–18).  
  * Marriage Certificate (if applicable).  
* **Health and Education Records:**  
  * School enrollment certificates or IDs for school-aged children.  
  * Health center records or immunization books for children aged 0–5 and pregnant women.  
* **Fees:** The registration and the issuance of the 4Ps ID and Cash Card are completely **FREE** of charge. No fees should be collected by anyone.

### **The Process**

**1.Wait for Official Selection and Notification:**  
Your family must first be assessed and selected through the CBMS database. Your barangay officials or City/Municipal Link (C/ML) will notify you if your household qualifies and will give you a schedule to attend a Community Assembly.

**2.Attend the Community Assembly and Validation:**  
Go to the designated registration site (usually a barangay hall, covered court, or LGU One-Stop Shop). A DSWD social worker will conduct a final assessment interview to validate your household's socio-demographic information and confirm your eligibility.

**3.Authenticate Identity:**  
Present your PhilSys National ID and other supporting documents. Your identity will be authenticated on-site to ensure accurate data entry into the Pantawid Pamilya Information System (PPIS).

**4.Sign the Oath of Commitment:**Required for program inclusion.  
Attend the mandatory orientation regarding the program's rules. You must sign the Oath of Commitment, legally agreeing to comply with the program’s conditions (e.g., maintaining an 85% school attendance rate for children, attending regular health check-ups, and attending Family Development Sessions).

**5.ID Processing and Issuance:**  
After you are officially enrolled, the DSWD Field Office (FO) processes your Certificate of Eligibility during the Post-Validation Phase. The physical 4Ps ID will be produced in batches and subsequently distributed to grantees through your assigned City/Municipal Link.

### **Estimated Timelines**

Because the 4Ps is a massive national program, the timeline is not immediate:

* **Assessment to Enrollment:** Validation and encoding into the system usually take **1 to 2 months** after the Community Assembly.  
* **ID and Cash Card Release:** The actual printing and distribution of the 4Ps ID (and the accompanying LandBank cash card for grants) are done in batches by the DSWD Regional Project Management Office. This can take anywhere from **2 to 6 months** after your official enrollment, depending on your region. While waiting, compliant beneficiaries are still credited their cash grants retroactively.

# Solo Parent ID

### **Prerequisites**

Before heading to your local government office, you must ensure you meet the strict legal definition of a solo parent—meaning you exclusively exercise sole parental care and support for a dependent child (up to 22 years old, unmarried, and unemployed) **without** any co-parenting or cohabitation arrangements.

Gather the following requirements (bring originals for verification and at least one photocopy of each):

* **Proof of Solo Parent Status:** The required document depends on your specific circumstance:  
  * *Widowed:* PSA Death Certificate of the spouse.  
  * *Separated/Abandoned:* Barangay Certification or Notarized Affidavit stating the fact and duration of separation or abandonment (must be at least one year).  
  * *Annulled:* Final court decree or church declaration of nullity.  
  * *Spouse Incapacity/Detention:* Medical certificate from a public doctor, or jail records showing detention of over one year.  
* **Proof of Filiation:** PSA Birth Certificate of your child or children.  
* **Proof of Residency:** Barangay Certificate of Residency certifying you have lived in your barangay for at least six (6) months.  
* **Proof of Income:** Income Tax Return (ITR) or a Certificate of Indigency from the barangay. *(Note: This is crucial because solo parents earning ₱250,000 or less annually qualify for a 10% discount on baby necessities and a ₱1,000 monthly cash subsidy).*  
* **ID Photos:** Two (2) recent 1x1 or 2x2 colored ID pictures with a white background.  
* **Application Form:** The official Solo Parent Registration Form (available for free at your local LGU office).  
* **Fees:** The application, issuance, and renewal of the Solo Parent ID are completely **FREE** of charge by law.

### **The Process**

**1.Obtain and Accomplish the Application Form:**  
Visit the Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) or the dedicated Solo Parents Office (SPO) at your City or Municipal Hall to secure the application form. *(Note: Highly urbanized cities like Quezon City allow you to download and submit this form via their online e-services portals).*

**2.Submit Documents:**  
Submit your fully accomplished form along with all your documentary requirements to the receiving social worker. They will log your application and issue an acknowledgment receipt.

**3.Undergo Assessment and Interview:**  
A licensed social worker will conduct a brief intake interview to verify your circumstances. They must ensure that you are indeed raising the child alone, as the law strictly disqualifies those with co-parenting setups or those receiving substantial support from a former partner.

**4.Wait for LGU Verification:**Validation phase.  
The LSWDO will validate your documents. In some cases, the social worker may conduct a quick background check or collateral verification with your barangay to confirm your solo parent status.

**5.Claim Your Solo Parent ID:**  
Once approved, return to the LSWDO on the date specified to claim your Solo Parent Identification Card (SPIC) and your Solo Parent Booklet (used to track your discounts and subsidies).

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time:** Under normal circumstances, the assessment and printing process takes **7 to 15 working days**, depending on the LGU's volume of applicants. Some LGUs officially state a maximum of **30 days** if deeper verification is required.  
* **Validity:** The Solo Parent ID is valid for **one (1) year** and must be renewed annually to ensure the parent's status has not changed (e.g., getting married or entering a co-parenting setup invalidates the ID).

## **LERIS Account Registration *(Required for PRC ID Application)***

Prerequisites

Before creating your LERIS account, prepare the following:

Personal details for registration: Your full legal name exactly as it appears on your PSA Birth Certificate, date of birth, sex, civil status, complete home address, and nationality.

Active email address: A valid, personal email address you have full access to. This becomes your permanent LERIS login credential and the address where PRC sends appointment confirmations, payment receipts, and official notices.

Active mobile number: A Philippine mobile number currently in your possession for OTP verification during account creation.

PSA Birth Certificate details: Your birthdate and birthplace as registered with the PSA — used for identity verification during registration.

Board examination details: Your name, the profession you are registering for, and the examination you passed. These are needed when completing your registration profile.

Fees: Creating a LERIS account is completely FREE of charge. Registration and examination fees are separate and apply only when filing for licensure examination or initial registration.

---

The Process

1. Go to online.prc.gov.ph and click Register or Create Account on the homepage.  
2. Fill out the online registration form with your complete personal details — full legal name, date of birth, sex, civil status, complete address, email address, and mobile number. Ensure your name matches your PSA Birth Certificate exactly, including middle name, suffixes, and special characters.  
3. Create your login credentials. Set a username and a strong password. Your registered email address will also serve as an alternative login.  
4. Check your email for a verification link. PRC will send an activation email to your registered address. Click the link to verify and activate your account. If the email does not appear within a few minutes, check your spam or junk folder.  
5. Log in to your newly activated LERIS account and complete your profile — upload a profile photo and confirm your contact details.  
6. Your LERIS account is now active. You may proceed to book an appointment for Initial Registration, pay the registration fee online, and download your Oath Form (Panunumpa ng Propesyonal) once payment is confirmed.

---

Estimated Timelines

Account registration: 10 to 20 minutes to complete the online form.

Email verification link delivery: Typically arrives within 1 to 5 minutes. If not received after 10 minutes, use the Resend Verification option on the portal.

Account activation: Immediate upon clicking the email verification link.

Appointment slot availability: Slots fill up quickly, especially in Metro Manila PRC offices. Book your appointment as early as possible — ideally within the first few days of results release when new slots are opened.

## **Community Tax Certificate / Cedula *(Required for PRC ID Application)***

Prerequisites

Before going to your city or municipal hall to obtain a Cedula, prepare the following:

Valid government-issued ID: Any ID for identity verification at the Community Tax window (e.g., Voter's ID, PhilSys National ID, Driver's License, or school ID for recent graduates).

Proof of income or tax details (if employed or earning):

* For employed individuals: Latest payslip or Certificate of Employment showing your monthly or annual salary  
* For self-employed individuals: Latest ITR or a declaration of your gross annual receipts  
* For non-earning individuals (students, unemployed): A simple verbal or written declaration of no income — the basic community tax rate applies

Knowledge of your current address: The Cedula is issued by the LGU where you are currently residing. Make sure to get it from the city or municipality where you actually live, not where you work or study.

Fee: The Cedula fee is composed of a basic tax plus an additional tax based on income. The basic community tax is ₱5.00. The additional tax is ₱1.00 for every ₱1,000 of income, real property, or receipts from the previous year. For most fresh board exam passers with no income or minimal income, the total fee is typically ₱5.00 to ₱100.00.

---

The Process

1. Go to the City or Municipal Treasurer's Office of the LGU where you currently reside. In some cities, Cedulas are also issued at barangay halls — confirm with your barangay if this option is available in your area.  
2. Proceed to the Community Tax Certificate window. Inform the clerk you need a Cedula for the current year. Get a queue number if required.  
3. Fill out the Cedula request slip provided on-site. Enter your full name, complete address, date of birth, civil status, citizenship, occupation, and income details.  
4. Present your valid ID to the clerk for identity verification.  
5. Declare your income or source of livelihood. The clerk will compute your community tax based on your declaration. For fresh graduates or unemployed applicants, declare zero income — only the basic ₱5.00 tax applies in most cases.  
6. Pay the computed community tax at the cashier. Keep your official receipt.  
7. Receive your Community Tax Certificate (Cedula). The document will bear your name, address, CTC number, date of issue, and official stamp. This is valid for the entire calendar year in which it is issued.

---

Estimated Timelines

Processing time: Same day. The entire visit typically takes 15 to 45 minutes depending on the queue at the Treasurer's Office.

Validity: The Cedula is valid only for the current calendar year (January 1 to December 31). If your PRC appointment falls in a new calendar year, you must obtain a new Cedula before your scheduled date.

Important Note: PRC specifically requires the Cedula to be valid for the current year at the time of your appointment. Do not use a Cedula issued in a previous year. If you are applying near the end of the year (October to December), be aware that your Cedula may expire before your appointment date if slots are delayed — plan accordingly.

**Integrated Bar of the Philippines (IBP) ID**

**Signing of the Roll of Attorneys *(To Obtain an Active Roll Number)***

Prerequisites

Before you can sign the Roll of Attorneys and receive your Roll Number, you must have completed all post-Bar requirements set by the Supreme Court. Prepare the following:

Official notice from the Supreme Court: After Bar results are released, the SC publishes the list of passers in the Official Gazette and on the SC website. Passers receive an official notice or are contacted through their registered address regarding the scheduled oath-taking ceremony.

Certificate of Passing / SC Clearance: The Supreme Court issues internal clearances before allowing a passer to participate in the oath-taking and Roll signing. Ensure you have no pending deficiencies in your law school requirements submitted to the SC.

Valid government-issued ID: One (1) primary ID for identity verification on the day of the ceremony.

PSA Birth Certificate: Required for final verification of identity against Bar examination records.

Appropriate attire for the oath-taking ceremony:

* Male lawyers: Barong Tagalog (traditional formal Filipino dress shirt) or business formal  
* Female lawyers: Formal business attire or the equivalent

IBP Chapter pre-assignment (for some batches): Before or during the oath-taking process, new lawyers are assigned to an IBP Chapter based on their home address or preferred region. You may be required to coordinate with your designated IBP Chapter beforehand. Confirm with the IBP National Office whether pre-assignment is required for your batch.

Fees: The oath-taking ceremony and Roll of Attorneys signing are FREE of charge. No fee is collected by the Supreme Court for these steps.

---

The Process

1. Monitor the official release of Bar Examination results. Results are announced by the Supreme Court, typically between November and April of the following year after the October Bar. Check the SC website (sc.judiciary.gov.ph) and the Official Gazette for your name on the list of passers.  
2. Await official communication from the Supreme Court regarding the scheduled oath-taking ceremony. The SC sends notices to passers through their registered address on file with the SC. The ceremony is typically held within weeks to a few months after results release, at a venue designated by the SC (usually a major arena or function hall for large batches).  
3. Coordinate with your assigned IBP Chapter. Before the ceremony, the IBP National Office or your SC notice will indicate the IBP Chapter you are assigned to. Contact that chapter to confirm your pre-registration, obtain any additional instructions, and inquire about your chapter dues schedule.  
4. Attend the Lawyer's Oath-Taking Ceremony. Appear on the designated date, time, and venue in proper attire. You will take the Lawyer's Oath before the Supreme Court en banc (all Justices present) or their designated representative.  
5. Sign the Roll of Attorneys. Immediately after taking the oath, each passer signs the Roll of Attorneys — the official registry of all lawyers in the Philippines maintained by the Supreme Court. Your signature in the Roll formalizes your admission to the Philippine Bar.  
6. Receive your Roll Number. Your Roll Number is assigned sequentially at the time of signing. This number is permanent and unique to you for life. Keep it on record as it is required for all IBP and legal practice transactions.  
7. Obtain your Certificate of Membership in the Philippine Bar. The Supreme Court or IBP issues this certificate confirming your admission. Keep a digital copy as required for IBP ID applications.

---

Estimated Timelines

Bar results release: Typically 6 to 8 months after the October Bar Examinations.

Oath-taking ceremony scheduling: Usually announced within 1 to 3 months after results release. The SC sets the date and it cannot be individually scheduled.

Roll Number issuance: On the day of the ceremony itself, at the time of signing.

Important Note: You cannot practice law in the Philippines, represent clients, sign pleadings, or apply for an IBP ID until you have taken the Lawyer's Oath and signed the Roll of Attorneys. The Roll Number is the definitive proof of your admission to the Bar.

**IBP Membership and Annual Dues Payment *(Required for Good Standing Status)***

Prerequisites

IBP membership is automatic and mandatory for all lawyers upon signing the Roll of Attorneys — you do not separately apply to become an IBP member. However, maintaining Good Standing status requires the annual payment of IBP dues to your assigned chapter. Prepare the following:

Your IBP Roll Number: Required for all transactions with your IBP Chapter.

Your assigned IBP Chapter: Determined at the time of oath-taking based on your home address or preferred region. If you are unsure of your chapter, contact the IBP National Office at ibp.ph or call their main line.

Knowledge of your outstanding dues balance: New lawyers begin accruing dues from the year of their admission. If applying in the middle of a year, confirm with your chapter whether dues are prorated or billed for the full year.

Payment method: IBP chapter dues vary by chapter but typically range from ₱2,000 to ₱5,000 per year. National dues are standardized; local chapter dues may vary. Accepted payment methods depend on the chapter — most accept cash, bank deposit, GCash, or Maya. Confirm with your chapter's treasurer.

Fees: IBP annual dues are set by the IBP Board of Governors and may be updated periodically. Confirm the current rate with your chapter. Penalties apply for late payment — unpaid dues result in loss of good standing and suspension from law practice.

---

The Process

1. Identify your IBP Chapter. Check the IBP website (ibp.ph) or contact the IBP National Office to confirm which chapter you are assigned to and obtain the chapter's contact details and treasurer's information.  
2. Contact your IBP Chapter to confirm your dues balance. Inquire about the current annual dues rate, any outstanding arrears from previous years, and accepted payment methods. New admittees should ask about the computation for their first year of dues.  
3. Pay your IBP annual dues. Settle the full amount through your chapter's accepted payment channels. If paying via bank deposit or online transfer, retain your deposit slip or transaction screenshot as proof of payment.  
4. Obtain your Official Receipt from the IBP Chapter. This receipt is the primary proof of your dues payment and Good Standing status. Keep the original and make at least one photocopy.  
5. Request a Certificate of Good Standing if needed separately. For IBP ID applications, your Good Standing status is verified internally through the IBP system. However, some courts and government agencies require a formal Certificate of Good Standing issued by your chapter — request this from your chapter treasurer or secretary at the same time as your dues payment if you anticipate needing it.  
6. Maintain annual dues payment. Good Standing must be renewed every year. IBP chapters typically collect dues between January and March of each year. Monitor renewal announcements from your chapter to avoid lapses.

---

Estimated Timelines

Dues payment processing: Immediate for cash or GCash/Maya payments. Bank deposit verification may take 1 to 3 working days.

Official receipt issuance: Same day for walk-in payments at the chapter office. Mailed receipts may take 3 to 7 working days.

Good Standing status update in the IBP system: 1 to 5 working days after payment is posted and verified by the chapter treasurer.

Important Note: The Supreme Court enforces IBP Good Standing as a requirement for continued law practice. Under Bar Matter No. 793, a lawyer who fails to pay IBP dues for two consecutive years is automatically dropped from the Roll of Attorneys and cannot practice law until reinstated. Reinstatement requires payment of all arrears, penalties, and a petition to the Supreme Court.

TIN ID 

**ORUS Account Registration \+ TIN Application *(For First-Time Taxpayers)***

Prerequisites

Before registering on ORUS as a first-time taxpayer, prepare the following:

Active personal email address: A valid email you have full access to. This becomes your permanent ORUS login credential and the address BIR uses for all official communications, including your TIN issuance confirmation.

Active mobile number: A Philippine mobile number for OTP verification during account creation.

Valid government-issued ID (digital copy): A clear photo or scan of any one of the following primary IDs:

* PhilSys National ID or ePhilID  
* Philippine Passport  
* Driver's License  
* UMID (SSS or GSIS)  
* Voter's ID  
* PRC ID

Personal details matching your ID: Full legal name exactly as it appears on your ID, date of birth, sex, civil status, complete home address, and citizenship.

Taxpayer category — know which type applies to you:

| Category | Who This Covers |
| :---- | :---- |
| Employed (Form 1902\) | New hires in private or government employment |
| Self-Employed / Professional (Form 1901\) | Freelancers, doctors, lawyers, business owners |
| Non-Resident (Form 1904\) | Foreign nationals, one-time transaction filers |
| Estate or Trust (Form 1903\) | Estates of deceased persons, trusts |

For Employed Applicants (additional requirement):

* Your employer's BIR-registered Employer TIN — your HR or payroll department provides this. Note: Many employers register their new hires' TINs directly through the BIR eReg system or ORUS Employer Portal on your behalf. Confirm with HR before self-registering to avoid duplicate TIN issuance, which is illegal under the National Internal Revenue Code.

For Self-Employed / Professional Applicants (additional requirements):

* DTI Certificate of Business Name Registration, SEC Certificate of Registration, or PRC License as proof of business or professional practice  
* Proof of business address (lease contract, utility bill, or mayor's permit)

Fees: TIN registration and ORUS account creation are completely FREE of charge.

---

The Process

1. Go to orus.bir.gov.ph and click Create Account on the homepage.  
2. Select your taxpayer type. Choose the appropriate category — Individual (for employees, professionals, and self-employed) or Non-Individual (for corporations and partnerships). For most first-time applicants, select Individual.  
3. Fill out the online registration form. Enter your complete personal details — full legal name exactly as on your ID, date of birth, sex, civil status, home address, email address, and mobile number. Select your taxpayer category (Employed, Self-Employed, etc.).  
4. Upload a clear photo or scan of your valid ID when prompted. Ensure the image is legible, unobstructed, and shows all four corners of the ID.  
5. For employed applicants: Enter your employer's TIN in the field provided. This links your registration to your employer's account so contributions and withholding taxes are properly tracked.  
6. Submit the registration form. The system will send a verification link to your registered email address.  
7. Check your email and click the verification link to confirm your email address and activate your ORUS account. If the email does not arrive within 5 minutes, check your spam or junk folder.  
8. Log in to your ORUS account. Your TIN will be displayed in your account dashboard upon successful registration. This is your permanent TIN — note it down and keep it secure. Possessing more than one TIN is a criminal offense in the Philippines.  
9. Proceed to apply for your Digital TIN ID directly from your ORUS account dashboard once your TIN is active.

---

Estimated Timelines

Account registration and TIN issuance: Immediate to same day for online ORUS registration. Your TIN appears in your dashboard upon account activation.

Email verification link delivery: Typically arrives within 1 to 5 minutes.

Total time: The entire ORUS registration process can be completed in 15 to 30 minutes.

Important Note: If the system flags a potential duplicate TIN or cannot verify your identity automatically, your application will be placed under manual review by BIR. In this case, you may be contacted via email to submit additional documents or visit your Revenue District Office (RDO) in person. Manual review cases typically take 3 to 7 working days.

Prerequisites

If you already have a TIN from previous employment, self-employment, or a prior BIR transaction, you do not need to apply for a new TIN. You only need to create an ORUS account to access the Digital TIN ID. Prepare the following:

Knowledge of your registered RDO: Your Revenue District Office number — this is the BIR office where your TIN is currently registered. If unsure, contact the BIR Contact Center or check your previous ITR or BIR Form 2316\.

Fees: ORUS account creation is completely FREE of charge.

---

The Process

1. Go to orus.bir.gov.ph and click Create Account.  
2. Select Individual as your taxpayer type and choose the option for existing taxpayers or TIN holders.  
3. Enter your existing TIN and personal details — full legal name, date of birth, and registered address — exactly as they appear in your BIR records. Mismatched details will prevent account creation.  
4. Upload a clear photo or scan of your valid ID for identity verification.  
5. Enter your email address and create a password. Your email becomes your ORUS login username.  
6. Verify your email address by clicking the activation link sent to your inbox.  
7. Log in to your ORUS account. Your existing TIN, registered RDO, and taxpayer information will be displayed in your dashboard.  
8. Proceed to apply for your Digital TIN ID from the dashboard.

---

Estimated Timelines

Account creation: 10 to 20 minutes for the online process.

Email verification: Immediate to within 5 minutes of submission.

Account activation: Immediate upon clicking the email verification link.

Important Note: If your personal details on ORUS do not match what is on file with your RDO — due to a name change, address update, or data entry errors from old registrations — your account creation will fail. You will need to visit your registered RDO in person with your valid ID and PSA Birth Certificate to have your records corrected before you can successfully create an ORUS account. Bring your TIN confirmation document (old TIN card, BIR Form 1902/1901, or any official BIR document bearing your TIN) to the RDO visit.

SEAMAN

**NBI Clearance *(Required for Seafarer's Record Book)***

Prerequisites

Before applying for an NBI Clearance, prepare the following:

Active email address: A valid, personal email address for account registration on the NBI online portal and for receiving your appointment confirmation and clearance status updates.

Active mobile number: A Philippine mobile number for OTP verification during account creation and appointment booking.

Valid government-issued ID (at least two): The NBI requires two valid IDs for identity verification on the day of your appointment. Accepted IDs include:

* PhilSys National ID  
* Philippine Passport  
* Driver's License  
* UMID (SSS or GSIS)  
* Voter's ID  
* PRC ID  
* PhilHealth ID  
* Postal ID  
* School ID (for students)

Fee: ₱115.00 for the NBI Clearance itself. Additional convenience fees apply depending on payment method:

* GCash or PayMaya: ₱20 to ₱25  
* Over-the-counter bank payment: ₱25 to ₱50  
* Debit/credit card: variable depending on bank

Optional delivery fee: If you opt for home delivery instead of walk-in claiming, an additional courier fee applies — typically ₱130 to ₱200 depending on your location.

---

The Process

1. Go to clearance.nbi.gov.ph and click Register to create an account. Enter your full legal name, date of birth, email address, and mobile number. Verify your email address by clicking the activation link sent to your inbox.  
2. Log in to your NBI account and click Apply for Clearance. Fill out the online application form with your complete personal details — full name, date of birth, birthplace, civil status, address, height, weight, and contact information. Ensure your name matches your valid IDs exactly.  
3. Select your purpose. For the Seafarer's Record Book, select Employment Abroad or Maritime/MARINA as the purpose of your clearance. This determines the type of clearance issued and the applicable fee.  
4. Choose your preferred NBI satellite office or regional office for your appointment. Select your appointment date and time from the available slots. Click to confirm.  
5. Pay the clearance fee through your chosen payment channel — GCash, Maya, credit/debit card, or over-the-counter at accredited payment centers (7-Eleven, Bayad Center, LBC, etc.). Keep your payment confirmation and reference number.  
6. Appear on your scheduled appointment date. Go to your chosen NBI office on time. Bring your payment reference number, appointment confirmation, and your two valid IDs (originals).  
7. Proceed to the biometrics and encoding station. Present your IDs and appointment confirmation to the officer. Your fingerprints and photo will be captured digitally on-site.  
8. Wait for name hit verification. The system will check if your name matches any records in the NBI database. If there is a name hit (i.e., your name matches a record on file), you will be directed to a verification window for additional screening — this does not necessarily mean you have a criminal record, as common names often generate false hits.  
9. Claim your NBI Clearance. If cleared without a name hit, the clearance is typically printed and released the same day. If there was a name hit that was resolved, release may take a few hours. For deliveries, your clearance will be mailed to your registered address.

---

Estimated Timelines

Account registration and appointment booking: 15 to 30 minutes online.

Appointment slot availability: Slots in Metro Manila offices fill quickly — book at least 1 to 2 weeks in advance. Provincial offices typically have more available slots.

On-site processing: 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on queue and whether a name hit occurs.

Same-day release: Available for cleared applications with no name hit complications.

Name hit cases: Resolution and release may take the same day up to 5 working days depending on the complexity of the verification.

Delivery option: 3 to 7 working days after clearance is issued.

Validity of NBI Clearance: One (1) year from the date of issuance. Ensure your clearance will still be valid on the date of your SRB appointment.

## **Basic Safety Training (BST) Certificate *(Required for Seafarer's Record Book)***

Prerequisites

Before enrolling in a Basic Safety Training course, prepare the following:

Pre-Medical Examination (Fit-for-Sea Medical Certificate): A medical examination conducted by a POEA-accredited or MARINA-recognized physician certifying that you are physically and mentally fit for sea duty. This is typically required by the training center before enrollment is finalized. The examination covers vision, hearing, cardiovascular health, and general physical fitness.

The medical exam typically includes:

* Complete Physical Examination  
* Chest X-ray  
* ECG (Electrocardiogram)  
* Complete Blood Count (CBC)  
* Urinalysis  
* Drug test  
* Fees range from ₱1,500 to ₱3,500 depending on the clinic and package

PSA Birth Certificate: Original and photocopy for identity verification during enrollment.

Valid government-issued ID: One (1) primary ID (original and photocopy).

Passport-sized ID photos: Typically two (2) to four (4) 1x1 or 2x2 photos depending on the training center's requirements.

For applicants who already hold a Seaman's Book or MARINA records: Your existing Seafarer's Record Book or MARINA-issued documents if re-enrolling or upgrading certificates.

Training fee: BST course fees vary by MARINA-accredited training center and location. Typical ranges:

* Private MARINA-accredited training centers: ₱8,000 to ₱15,000  
* MAAP (Maritime Academy of Asia and the Pacific) and PMMA (Philippine Merchant Marine Academy) affiliated centers: rates may vary  
* Some maritime schools offer BST as part of their regular curriculum for maritime students

---

The Process

1. Choose a MARINA-accredited training center. Only training centers accredited by the Maritime Industry Authority (MARINA) are authorized to issue BST Certificates valid for STCW compliance. Verify accreditation status at the MARINA website (marina.gov.ph) before enrolling to ensure your certificate will be accepted.  
2. Complete your pre-medical examination. Visit a POEA-accredited clinic or MARINA-recognized physician for your Fit-for-Sea medical examination. Bring your valid ID. Once cleared, you will receive a Medical Certificate stating you are fit for sea duty. This certificate is required before the training center will confirm your enrollment.  
3. Contact or visit your chosen training center to inquire about enrollment schedules. BST courses run in batches — confirm the next available start date, the exact list of requirements, and the applicable fee. Some centers allow online enrollment; others require walk-in registration.  
4. Submit your enrollment requirements and pay the training fee. Submit your PSA Birth Certificate, valid ID, ID photos, and Medical Certificate to the training center's registrar. Pay the course fee and obtain your Official Receipt (OR) — this is a separate prerequisite for the SRB application itself and must be kept safe.  
5. Attend and complete the BST course. The Basic Safety Training program covers four core competency modules as required by the STCW (Standards of Training, Certification, and Watchkeeping for Seafarers) Convention:  
   * Personal Survival Techniques  
   * Fire Prevention and Fire Fighting  
   * Elementary First Aid  
   * Personal Safety and Social Responsibilities  
6. The course typically runs for 4 to 5 consecutive days of classroom instruction, practical drills, and demonstrations.  
7. Pass all required assessments and practical evaluations. Each module includes both written assessments and hands-on practical evaluations. You must pass all components to qualify for certification.  
8. Receive your BST Certificate. Upon successfully completing and passing all modules, the training center will issue your MARINA-endorsed Basic Safety Training Certificate. Ensure the certificate bears the MARINA accreditation stamp and the training center's official seal.

---

Estimated Timelines

Pre-medical examination: Same day to 1 to 2 working days for results depending on the clinic.

Training center enrollment processing: Same day to 2 working days after submission of requirements.

BST course duration: 4 to 5 consecutive days of training.

Certificate release: Typically on the last day of training or within 1 to 3 working days after completion and assessment.

Total estimated time (from medical exam to certificate): Approximately 1 to 2 weeks end-to-end.

Validity of BST Certificate: The BST Certificate is valid for five (5) years from the date of issuance in line with STCW amendments. Refresher training is required before expiry to maintain validity.

Important Note: Ensure your BST Certificate is still valid at the time of your SRB appointment and for the foreseeable duration of your intended employment contract. An expired BST Certificate will result in rejection of your SRB application.

OWWA

**Overseas Employment Certificate (OEC) *(Required for OWWA e-Card and Airport Exit Clearance)***

Prerequisites

Before applying for an OEC, prepare the following:

Valid Philippine Passport: Original with at least 6 months of remaining validity.

POEA-processed or DMW-verified Employment Contract: Your employment contract must be verified and processed by the Department of Migrant Workers (DMW). Contracts processed by accredited recruitment agencies are typically already on file with the DMW.

Active OWWA Membership: Your OWWA membership must be current (paid and valid) before an OEC can be issued.

For New Hires:

* POEA-processed employment contract  
* Recruitment agency documents (if hired through an agency)  
* Visa or work permit for the destination country (if already available)

For Balik-Manggagawa (Rehires returning to the same employer):

* Previous OEC or proof of previous deployment  
* Proof of re-employment with the same employer (e.g., contract extension letter, employer certification, or job offer letter)  
* Previous passport or entry stamps showing prior deployment

Fees: The OEC itself is FREE of charge. However, a processing fee of ₱100.00 is collected for documentation. Balik-Manggagawa OEC (BM-OEC) exemptions processed online are also FREE.

---

The Process

Option A — Online OEC via DMW OEC Online System (For Balik-Manggagawa)

1. Go to the DMW Balik-Manggagawa OEC portal at bm.dmw.gov.ph and log in to your account, or create one if you are a first-time online user.  
2. Fill out the BM-OEC application form online. Enter your passport details, employer and contract information, and deployment country. The system will verify your previous OEC and employment records against the DMW database.  
3. Confirm your OWWA membership status. The system checks your active OWWA membership automatically. If your membership has lapsed, you must renew it before the OEC can be processed.  
4. Submit the application. If your records are verified and complete, the system will generate your BM Exemption Number or issue your OEC digitally.  
5. Download and print your OEC or note your BM Exemption Number. Present this at the airport immigration counter upon departure as your exit clearance.

---

Option B — Walk-In at a DMW Office (For New Hires)

1. Go to the nearest DMW Regional Office or POEA satellite office with your complete documents.  
2. Proceed to the OEC processing window and submit your passport, POEA-processed employment contract, and OWWA membership receipt for verification.  
3. Pay the ₱100.00 documentation processing fee at the cashier and keep your official receipt.  
4. Wait for OEC encoding and printing. The officer will verify your documents against DMW records and encode your OEC details.  
5. Receive and review your OEC. Confirm that your name, passport number, employer details, and deployment country are all correct. The OEC serves as your airport exit clearance and exempts you from travel tax and terminal fee.

---

Estimated Timelines

Online BM-OEC application: 15 to 30 minutes if all records are on file. BM Exemption Number is generated immediately upon approval.

Walk-in OEC processing: 1 to 2 hours on-site depending on queue volume.

Validity of OEC: Valid for the specific deployment trip it is issued for — it is tied to your departure date and destination. A new OEC must be obtained for each trip.

Important Note: The OEC doubles as your airport exit clearance and exempts you from the travel tax (₱1,620) and terminal fee (₱550) at Philippine international airports. Present it at the airline check-in counter or immigration upon departure. Without a valid OEC or BM Exemption Number, you will be required to pay the full travel tax and terminal fee before boarding.

## **OWWA Membership Registration *(Active Membership Required for e-Card)***

Prerequisites

Before registering for or renewing your OWWA membership, prepare the following:

Valid Philippine Passport: Original with at least 6 months of remaining validity. This is the primary identity document for all OWWA transactions.

This document legally certifies the rightful dependents of the active or retired military personnel. The military member (the principal) usually initiates this, but dependents can process it on their behalf with the proper authorization.

#### Prerequisites

Before submitting your request to JAGO or PGMC, you must compile a folder containing the following:

  * PSA Marriage Certificate.  
  * PSA Advisory on Marriages (CENOMAR/CEMAR) for both the military personnel and the spouse.  
* Proof of Filiation: PSA Birth Certificates of all declared children (or dependent parents, if the personnel is single).  
* Military Records: Authenticated copy of the personnel's Statement of Service (SOS) or Retirement/Separation Orders (request this from the Major Service Adjutant—HPA for Army, HPN for Navy, HPAF for Air Force, or GHQ).

1.Draft and Notarize the Affidavit:

The military personnel must execute an "Affidavit of Legal Beneficiary." Have this document notarized by a Notary Public or an authorized military administering officer.

2.Secure Unit Adjutant Endorsement:

Submit the notarized affidavit and all PSA documents to the military personnel’s Major Service Adjutant (or their current Unit Commander). They will verify the records and issue an initial "Certification of Legal Beneficiaries."

3.Submit to JAGO or PGMC:

Bring the complete folder—including the Adjutant's certification—to the Judge Advocate General’s Office (JAGO) or the Pension and Gratuity Management Center (PGMC) at Camp Aguinaldo (or the designated regional satellite office).

4.Verification and Issuance:

The legal officers will review the civil registry documents to ensure there are no conflicting claims (e.g., multiple marriages). Once cleared, they will issue the official, authenticated copy of the Declaration of Legal Beneficiaries.

#### Estimated Timelines

* Processing Time: Depending on the completeness of your civil registry documents and the backlog at the legal office, this usually takes 1 to 2 weeks to be issued.

## **Police Blotter *(Required When Replacing a Lost AFP Dependent ID)***

Prerequisites

Before filing a police blotter report, prepare the following:

Personal identification: Any valid government-issued ID or any document bearing your name and photo for the police officer's reference when recording your report.

Complete details of what was lost: The full name of the AFP Dependent ID holder, the approximate date and location of the loss, and a description of what was lost (AFP Dependent ID card). If you recall any identifying details of the card such as the ID number or issue date, include these in your account.

Narrative of the circumstances: A clear, truthful account of when, where, and how the ID was lost — whether misplaced, left somewhere, stolen, or lost in an incident. For theft cases, include as much detail as possible about the incident.

Fees: Filing a police blotter report is completely FREE of charge. No fee should be collected by the police for recording a blotter entry.

---

The Process

1. Go to the nearest Philippine National Police (PNP) station in the city or municipality where the loss occurred. If the exact location of the loss is unknown, file the blotter at the police station nearest to your home address or where you first noticed the loss.  
2. Proceed to the Police Community Precinct desk or the Desk Officer on duty. Inform the officer that you need to file a blotter report for a lost government ID — specifically an AFP Dependent ID.  
3. Provide your full account of the loss to the desk officer. State your complete name, address, contact number, and the full details of the lost item. Describe when you last had the ID, when and where you noticed it was missing, and the circumstances of the loss. Be as accurate and specific as possible.  
4. The desk officer will record your report in the police blotter. The police blotter is the official daily log of incidents recorded at the station. Your report will be entered with a blotter entry number, the date and time of reporting, and a summary of your account.  
5. Request a certified true copy or certified extract of the blotter entry. After the officer records the incident, ask for a printed copy of your blotter entry bearing the station's official stamp, the desk officer's signature, and the blotter entry number. This certified copy is what you will submit to the AFP personnel office.  
6. Keep your certified blotter copy safe. Make at least one photocopy before submitting the original to the AFP personnel office, as you will also need to submit an Affidavit of Loss alongside it.

---

Estimated Timelines

Filing and recording of blotter report: Same day — approximately 15 to 45 minutes depending on the queue at the police station and the desk officer's workload.

Issuance of certified blotter extract: Same day in most police stations immediately after the report is recorded. Some stations may ask you to return later in the day or the following morning for the certified copy if the station commander's signature is required for certification.

Total estimated time: The entire process can typically be completed within a single visit of 30 minutes to 1.5 hours.

Important Note: The police blotter is distinct from a formal complaint — it is simply an official record of a reported incident. Filing a blotter does not automatically trigger an investigation unless you additionally request one (e.g., if the ID was stolen). For AFP Dependent ID replacement purposes, the blotter entry is required simply to officially document the loss and protect you from any unauthorized use of the missing ID. The AFP personnel office will require both the police blotter and a notarized Affidavit of Loss — ensure both documents are prepared before submitting your replacement application.

## **PVAO Pension Enrollment *(Active Pension Status — Membership Requirement)***

Prerequisites

PVAO pension enrollment differs depending on whether you are the veteran or a qualified beneficiary/dependent. Prepare the following based on your category:

For Veterans (Direct Pensioners):

* DD Form 214 or Discharge Papers: Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty, or any official military service record issued by the AFP or the U.S. Armed Forces (for WWII veterans and recognized guerrilla fighters)  
* PSA Birth Certificate: Original SECPA copy for identity and age verification  
* Marriage Certificate (if applicable): PSA-certified original  
* Service Record: Authenticated copy of your military service record from the AFP Office of the Adjutant General (OTAG) or equivalent records office  
* Affidavit of Participation (for WWII guerrilla veterans): A notarized affidavit attesting to your participation in recognized guerrilla units, supported by unit rosters or corroborating affidavits from fellow guerrilla members or their heirs  
* Recognition document: Certificate of Recognition from the AFP or PVAO confirming your status as a recognized veteran  
* Valid government-issued ID: One (1) primary ID for identity verification  
* Two (2) recent 2x2 ID photos: White background, no eyeglasses

For Qualified Beneficiaries/Dependents (Surviving Spouse, Children, or Parents):

* PSA Death Certificate of the veteran: Confirming the veteran's passing  
* PSA Marriage Certificate: For surviving spouse applicants  
* PSA Birth Certificate of the veteran and of the dependent: To establish relationship  
* PVAO Pension Identification of the deceased veteran (if available)  
* Proof that no other qualified beneficiary exists with a higher priority claim: An affidavit or PVAO-issued clearance confirming beneficiary hierarchy (spouse takes priority over children; children over parents)  
* Certificate of No Re-marriage (CENOMAR): For surviving spouse applicants, to confirm the spouse has not re-married — re-marriage disqualifies a surviving spouse from PVAO pension  
* School records or enrollment certificate: For dependent children below 21 years old still in school  
* Medical certificate of incapacity: For dependent children over 21 who are physically or mentally incapacitated  
* Valid government-issued ID of the beneficiary  
* Two (2) recent 2x2 ID photos

Fees: PVAO pension enrollment and all related processing are completely FREE of charge.

---

The Process

For Veterans

1. Secure your military service and discharge records. Go to the AFP Office of the Adjutant General (OTAG) at Camp Aguinaldo, Quezon City, or the nearest AFP command headquarters to request an authenticated copy of your service record and discharge papers. For WWII veterans or guerrilla fighters, coordinate with the PVAO Records Division directly for verification of wartime service.  
2. Secure recognition from the AFP (if not yet recognized). Veterans who have not been formally recognized must file a petition for recognition with the AFP through the OTAG. Submit your service records, affidavit of participation, and corroborating documents. The AFP will conduct a verification process and issue a Certificate of Recognition upon approval.  
3. Go to the PVAO Main Office at the PVAO Building, General Luna corner Cadena de Amor Streets, Bagong Lipunan ng Crame, Cubao, Quezon City, or the nearest PVAO Regional Extension Unit (REU). Proceed to the Claims and Benefits Division or the Pension Processing section.  
4. Submit a complete pension application. Present all required documents — service record, discharge papers, AFP Recognition Certificate, PSA Birth Certificate, and valid ID. The PVAO officer will review your documents and assess your eligibility for pension under the applicable law (R.A. 6948 as amended, or other applicable veterans' legislation).  
5. Undergo PVAO interview and records verification. A PVAO officer will interview you to verify your service history and personal details. Your documents will be matched against PVAO's and AFP's records databases.  
6. Await PVAO Board review and approval. The PVAO Board of Administrators reviews applications and issues an approval order for pension claims that meet all eligibility criteria. This step involves internal review and may require additional documents or clarification.  
7. Receive your pension enrollment confirmation. Upon approval, you will be assigned a PVAO Pensioner number and notified of your pension category, monthly grant amount, and payment schedule. Your Active Pension Status is now established in the PVAO database.

---

For Beneficiaries/Dependents

1. Go to the PVAO Main Office or nearest REU and proceed to the Beneficiary Claims section. Inform the officer that you are filing as a surviving beneficiary of a deceased PVAO pensioner or recognized veteran.  
2. Submit all required documents — PSA Death Certificate of the veteran, your PSA Marriage Certificate or Birth Certificate establishing your relationship, CENOMAR (for surviving spouses), and proof of the veteran's PVAO pension enrollment if applicable.  
3. Undergo interview and beneficiary verification. The PVAO officer will verify your relationship to the veteran, confirm your priority standing in the beneficiary hierarchy, and check that no disqualifying conditions apply (e.g., re-marriage of spouse, or legitimate children existing for parent claimants).  
4. Await PVAO processing and Board approval. Beneficiary claims undergo the same Board review process as veteran applications. Additional documents may be requested during this stage.  
5. Receive beneficiary enrollment confirmation. Upon approval, your beneficiary status is recorded in the PVAO database. You are now eligible for the PVAO Pensioner's ID and monthly pension grants.

---

Estimated Timelines

AFP records retrieval and recognition: 1 to 6 months depending on the completeness of wartime records and AFP processing load. WWII guerrilla claims may take longer due to the age and scarcity of documentation.

PVAO pension application processing: 3 to 12 months from submission of complete documents, depending on the complexity of the claim and Board review schedules.

Beneficiary claim processing: 3 to 6 months for straightforward surviving spouse or child claims with complete documentation.

Important Note: PVAO pension enrollment is a one-time process. Once your Active Pension Status is established, you remain in the PVAO database permanently unless disqualified (e.g., a surviving spouse re-marries). Keep all PVAO correspondence and your pensioner number on record for all future transactions including ID applications.

## **Special Power of Attorney (SPA) *(For Representative Applicants)***

Prerequisites

Before visiting a notary public to have an SPA prepared, prepare the following:

Full legal name and details of the principal (the PVAO pensioner or beneficiary): Complete name, address, date of birth, and a valid ID number. The principal is the person granting authority — they must personally sign the SPA.

Full legal name and details of the authorized representative: Complete name, address, and valid ID of the person being authorized to act on the principal's behalf.

Specific authority being granted: For PVAO ID application purposes, the SPA must specifically state that the representative is authorized to apply for, process, and receive the PVAO Pensioner's ID on behalf of the principal. A general SPA may also be used if it broadly covers government transactions.

Valid ID of both the principal and the representative: One (1) primary government-issued ID each, presented before the notary for verification.

Notarial fee: Typically ₱200 to ₱500 depending on the notary public or law office. Indigent applicants may seek free notarization at the Public Attorney's Office (PAO).

---

The Process

1. Go to a notary public or law office. Commissioned notaries are found at law offices near courthouses, inside malls, or at city and municipal hall complexes. The PAO at your city or municipal hall also offers free notarization for qualified indigent clients.  
2. Request a Special Power of Attorney for PVAO ID application. Inform the notary of the specific purpose. The notary or their assistant will draft the SPA using the appropriate legal template, incorporating the names of the principal and representative and the specific authority being granted.  
3. Review the drafted SPA carefully. Before signing, confirm that the principal's full name and details are correct, the representative's name and details are accurate, the authority granted specifically covers the PVAO ID application process, and the document is properly formatted with a proper acknowledgment clause.  
4. The principal must sign the SPA in the physical presence of the notary. The person granting authority (the PVAO pensioner or beneficiary) must personally appear before the notary and sign. Present both the principal's and representative's valid IDs for the notary's records.  
5. Pay the notarial fee and receive the notarized SPA bearing the notary's official seal, signature, and complete notarial details — document number, page, book, and series year.  
6. Make at least one photocopy of the notarized SPA before submitting the original to the PVAO office. Keep one copy for your personal records.

---

Estimated Timelines

SPA drafting by the notary: 15 to 30 minutes using a standard template.

Notarization and release: Same day. The entire visit typically takes 30 minutes to 1 hour from arrival to claiming the document.

Important Note: The principal — the PVAO pensioner or beneficiary — must be physically present before the notary to sign. If the principal is bedridden or physically unable to travel to a notary office, some notaries offer home or hospital visits for an additional fee. Alternatively, the PVAO office may accept the separate arrangement of a postcard-sized photo of the applicant holding the front page of a current-day newspaper instead of personal appearance, as stated in the prerequisites — confirm with PVAO which option they require before proceeding.

# Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) ID

Here is the complete step-by-step guide to acquiring your first Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) ID—officially known as the Professional Identification Card (PIC)—for new board examination passers (Initial Registration).

The PRC has fully transitioned to an online appointment system through their Licensure Examination and Registration Information System (LERIS). Walk-ins without a confirmed LERIS appointment are strictly prohibited.

### **Prerequisites**

Before booking an appointment, ensure you have the following requirements ready. You will need to bring these to the PRC office on your scheduled date:

* **Active LERIS Account:** You must be registered on the PRC LERIS portal (online.prc.gov.ph).  
* **Oath Form (Panunumpa ng Propesyonal):** This form is automatically generated in your LERIS account after you pay the initial registration fee. It must be downloaded and printed.  
* **Notice of Admission (NOA):** Bring your original NOA (which contains your application number) and one photocopy.  
* **Community Tax Certificate (Cedula):** Must be valid for the current year. Bring the original and a photocopy.  
* **ID Photos:** Two (2) recent passport-sized photos with a plain white background and a complete name tag (Surname, First Name, Middle Name). You must wear collared and decent attire in the photo.  
* **Documentary Stamps:** Two (2) pieces of unused documentary stamps (can be purchased at the PRC premises or local post office).  
* **Fees:** Prepare for the online payment (via PayMaya, GCash, LandBank, or BancNet).  
  * **Baccalaureate Degrees** (e.g., CPA, RN, LPT): **₱1,050.00** (₱600 initial fee \+ ₱450 annual fee).  
  * **Non-Baccalaureate Degrees** (e.g., Master Electrician, Midwife): **₱870.00** (₱450 initial fee \+ ₱420 annual fee).  
  * *(Note: Additional convenience fees of around ₱15 to ₱50 may apply depending on your payment channel).*

### **The Process**

**1.Update Your LERIS Profile:**  
Log in to your PRC LERIS account. Update your personal details and ensure you upload a new 2x2 ID photo following the PRC's strict guidelines (white background, collared shirt, no glasses, ears visible). This uploaded photo is exactly what will be printed on your PRC ID card.

**2.Book an Initial Registration Appointment:**Requires your Application Number.  
On your dashboard, click **Select Transaction** and choose **Initial Registration**. Select your profession from the dropdown menu and enter the Application Number found on your NOA. Choose your preferred PRC Regional Office or Service Center, then select an available date and time slot.

**3.Pay the Fees Online:**  
Proceed to the payment gateway and choose your preferred method (GCash, PayMaya, LandBank, etc.). Once the payment is confirmed, your transaction status on LERIS will change to "Paid."

**4.Print the Oath Form:**  
Return to the "Existing Transactions" tab on your LERIS dashboard. Click the **Print Document** button to download your official Oath Form. Print this form; do not sign it yet, as it must be signed in front of a PRC administering officer.

**5.Submit Documents to the PRC Office:**Personal appearance is required.  
Go to your chosen PRC branch on the exact date and time of your appointment. Proceed to the Initial Registration or Customer Service window and submit your printed Oath Form, NOA, Cedula, ID photos, and documentary stamps.

**6.Sign the Registry Book:**  
Once an officer verifies your documents, you will be asked to sign and place your thumbmark on the official Registry of Professionals sheet. The officer will then give you a claim slip containing the schedule for your physical ID release.

**7.Attend the Oath-Taking Ceremony:**Mandatory for new professionals.  
You cannot officially practice your profession or claim your ID until you take your professional oath. Purchase an oath-taking ticket at the PRC office and attend the mass oath-taking ceremony (or a special/virtual oath-taking, if permitted by your specific board).

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing at the PRC Office:** Document submission and signing the registry book usually take **1 to 2 hours** depending on the queue on your appointment day.  
* **ID Releasing:** Your physical PRC ID and Certificate of Registration (COR) are generally released **after** you have attended the official oath-taking ceremony. Some professions distribute the IDs on the actual day of the mass oath-taking, while others require you to return to the PRC branch on the date indicated on your claim slip (usually **3 to 7 working days** after your oath-taking).  
* **Validity:** A newly issued PRC ID is valid for exactly **three (3) years**, expiring on your birth month.

# Integrated Bar of the Philippines (IBP) ID

Here is the step-by-step guide to applying for your Integrated Bar of the Philippines (IBP) ID.

As a lawyer in the Philippines, you can process this application either manually via email or digitally through the official **myIBP** mobile app, which is the highly recommended and faster route.

### **Prerequisites**

Before initiating the application, you must be a fully admitted lawyer and have the following ready:

* **Active Roll Number:** You must have already passed the Bar and signed the Roll of Attorneys at the Supreme Court. (For new passers, keep a soft copy of your Certificate of Membership in the Philippine Bar handy).  
* **Good Standing Status:** You must have zero outstanding/unpaid IBP annual dues and no pending or final disciplinary cases with the Commission on Bar Discipline (CBD).  
* **ID Photo:** A high-resolution 2x2 ID photo (white background, wearing a blazer/corporate attire or a Barong Tagalog).  
* **IBP Lawyers ID Form:** Only necessary if you are applying manually via email.  
* **Fees:**  
  * ID Fee: **₱350.00**  
  * Courier Fee (Optional, if you want it delivered): **₱200.00**  
  * *(Note: A minimal convenience/transaction fee of around ₱20 may apply depending on your payment channel).*

### **The Process**

**1.Update Your Dues:**  
Before requesting an ID, ensure all your annual IBP dues are fully paid. You can check your account balance and settle dues directly through the **myIBP** app.

**2.Submit the Application:**Choose between the app or email.

Once logged in, ensure your profile (especially your email address) is updated. You cannot generate a digital ID if your email is not verified or current.

**3.Upload Your Photo:**  
Navigate to the "Digital TIN ID" section. Upload your 1x1 white-background photo following the strict specifications mentioned in the prerequisites (front-facing, clear ears, no obstructions).

**4.Submit and Generate:**  
After uploading, the system will process your photo. Once approved, you will be able to view and download your Digital TIN ID directly from your ORUS profile.

**5.Optional: Sync to eGovPH App:**  
Download the **eGovPH app** from the App Store or Google Play Store. Log in using your eGovPH credentials; the app will pull your Digital TIN ID from the BIR database, allowing you to present it as a mobile ID wallet card.

* **Physical Card:** The BIR no longer emphasizes the issuance of the old laminated cardboard/plastic physical card. If you specifically require a physical card for a lost/damaged ID, you must visit your Revenue District Office (RDO) and pay a nominal fee (₱100), though the Digital TIN ID is legally sufficient for all government and private transactions.

# AFP Beneficiary ID / Military ID

Applying for an Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) Dependent ID is a formal administrative process handled directly through military personnel offices. Unlike civilian IDs, there is no public online portal for this application; it is typically processed through the unit where the military personnel is assigned.

### **Prerequisites**

You must gather the original documents and provide photocopies for submission. Requirements vary slightly based on the category of the military personnel (Active or Retired).

**Common Requirements:**

* **Duly Accomplished Application Form:** Obtain this from the military personnel's Unit Adjutant or the Office of the Adjutant General (OTAG).  
* **Valid Identification:** Your own government-issued ID (e.g., PhilSys National ID, Passport).  
* **Proof of Relationship (PSA-Certified Originals):**  
  * **Spouse:** PSA Marriage Contract and a recent PSA-certified Advisory on Marriages (CENOMAR/CEMAR).  
  * **Children:** PSA Birth Certificate.  
  * **Parents:** PSA Birth Certificate of the military personnel (only for indigent parents 60 years and above).  
* **Military Orders:** Authenticated copies of the personnel’s Retirement/Separation Orders or, for active personnel, their current Promotion or Re-enlistment Orders.  
* **AFP-Issued Certification:** An authenticated copy of the “Declaration of Legal Beneficiaries” from the Judge Advocate General’s Office (JAGO) or the Pension and Gratuity Management Center (PGMC).  
* **Photos:** Recent 2x2 ID pictures (usually 15 days old or less) with a white background in formal/semi-formal attire.  
* **If Replacing a Lost ID:** An Affidavit of Loss and a Police Blotter are mandatory.

### **The Process**

**1.Coordinate with the Military Unit:**  
The military personnel must coordinate with their **Unit Adjutant or Administration Officer**. They will provide the official, current version of the application form and verify the specific requirements for the unit’s records.

**2.Accomplish and Endorse the Form:**  
Fill out the application form accurately. The form must be signed and endorsed by the military personnel's **Unit Commander or Adjutant**, confirming the applicant is a legitimate dependent.

**3.Submit Documents to the Admin Office:**  
Submit the completed form along with all supporting documents to the designated military administrative office (e.g., OTAG, or the specific branch’s Adjutant office for the Army, Navy, or Air Force).

**4.Verification and Biometrics:**  
Personnel will verify the documents against military records. You will likely need to appear in person for biometric data capture (photograph, digital signature, and thumbprint) if your unit does not already have your file on record.

**5.Claim the ID:**  
Once processed and approved, the ID will be released by the Adjutant's office or the ID section. You will be required to sign the release logbook upon receipt.

### **Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time:** Since this is an internal military administrative procedure, the timeline varies by branch and current unit workload. Typically, it takes **1 to 4 weeks** from the date of submission to the release of the ID.  
* **Validity:** The ID is usually valid for a set term (often 1–3 years) and must be renewed, especially upon the promotion, retirement, or change in status of the military personnel.

**Important Note:** Do not trust online "fillable" templates from third-party websites or unofficial document services. Always secure the official, blank application form directly from the military personnel's unit to ensure the document is accepted by the Adjutant's office.

**Step 1: Online Registration and Profile Completion**

1. Visit the official National Police Clearance System (NPCS) website at pnpclearance.ph.  
2. Click on "Register" to create a new account using your personal information and active email address.  
3. Check your email for a verification link and click it to activate your account.  
4. Log in to your account, click "Edit," and fill out your complete profile details, including your address, civil status, and contact information.

1. Once your profile is complete, click the "Clearance Application" or "Clearance Appointment" button.  
2. Select the Police Station nearest to you or the one most convenient for you to visit.

**Step 5: Release of Police Clearance**

1. Wait for your name to be called.  
2. If your record is clear (meaning you have no "hit" or namesake with a derogatory record), your National Police Clearance will be printed and handed to you on the spot.

**Estimated Timelines:**

* **Processing Time:** If you have no "hit" in the criminal database, the clearance is released on the same day—often within 10 to 15 minutes after your biometrics are taken at the station. If you are flagged with a "hit," your application will require manual verification by the PNP, which typically takes 5 to 10 working days.  
* **Validity:** The National Police Clearance is generally valid for six (6) months from the date of issuance.

# CEdula

### **Step-by-Step Guide: Community Tax Certificate (Cedula)**

**Prerequisites**

Before heading to your local government office, prepare the following:

* **Valid Identification:** Bring at least one (1) original valid government-issued photo ID (e.g., PhilSys National ID, Passport, Driver's License, Voter's ID).  
* **Proof of Income (if applicable):** For employed individuals, bring a copy of your payslip, Certificate of Compensation, or BIR Form 2316\. For business owners, bring your approved business tax declaration. If you are unemployed or a student, you do not need this.  
* **Application Form:** The Community Tax Declaration Form (CTDF), which is provided for free at the issuing office.  
* **Fees:**  
  * **Basic Tax:** ₱5.00  
  * **Additional Tax:** ₱1.00 for every ₱1,000 of gross earnings or assessed real property value from the previous year.

**The Process**

1. **Step 1: Eligibility Check:** Ensure you meet the requirements. You must be at least 18 years old and a resident of the municipality or city where you are applying. You should also fall under any of these categories: regularly employed for at least 30 consecutive days, engaged in business, an owner of real property (assessed value of ₱1,000 or more), or required by law to file an income tax return.  
2. **Step 2: Documentary Requirements:** Go to the Treasurer's Office at your City Hall, Municipal Hall, or designated Barangay Hall. Secure the Community Tax Declaration Form (CTDF) and fill it out completely with your personal details and income information.  
3. **Step 3: Application and Assessment:** Submit the accomplished CTDF, your valid ID, and your proof of income (if applicable) to the assessment officer. They will evaluate your form and compute the exact community tax amount you need to pay based on your declared gross receipts or earnings.  
4. **Step 4: Biometrics Capture:** Proceed to the cashier to pay the assessed tax amount. After paying, you will be required to sign the copies of the certificate and affix your right thumbprint on the document using an ink pad.  
5. **Step 5: Claiming the Certificate:** The officer will officially sign and hand over the original copy of your Community Tax Certificate. Double-check your name, address, and the stated purpose for accuracy before leaving the counter.

**Estimated Timelines**

* **Processing Time:** Obtaining a Cedula is an over-the-counter transaction that is released on the same day. The entire process typically takes about **5 to 20 minutes**, depending on the queue at the local hall.  
* **Validity:** The Cedula is valid only for the calendar year it was issued and expires on December 31 of that same year.

Settle the corresponding LTOPF fee at Landbank (via the Link.BizPortal or over-the-counter). Log back in and upload your payment receipt to the system.  
5. **Step 5: Personal Appearance and Document Submission**  
   After your payment is confirmed, set an appointment date. Proceed to the FEO at Camp Crame or your designated Regional Civil Security Unit (RCSU). Bring all your original documents, test results, and receipts placed securely inside a long brown envelope clearly labeled with your full name.  
6. **Step 6: Biometrics and Releasing**  
   Submit your physical documents for final verification at the receiving window. Once cleared, you will undergo biometrics capture (if your data was not completely captured during your preliminary tests). Your physical LTOPF card will then be printed and issued to you. *(Note: You can now legally purchase a firearm, but the firearm itself must go through a separate registration process before you can take it home).*

**Estimated Timelines**

* **Clearance and Seminar Gathering:** It usually takes 1 to 3 days to complete the Neuro-Psychiatric exam, Drug Test, and Gun Safety Seminar.  
* **Online Assessment:** Expect around 3 to 7 working days, depending on the current volume of applications at the FEO.  
* **Card Issuance:** Upon submission of original documents and final verification at the physical FEO office, the ID card is typically printed and released on the same day.  
* **Validity:** You have the option to apply for an LTOPF validity of either five (5) or ten (10) years.

# Barangay clearance

Barangay clearance

### **Step-by-Step Guide: Barangay ID / Clearance**

**Prerequisites:**

Before heading to your local Barangay Hall, ensure you have the following requirements ready:

* **Valid Identification:** At least one (1) valid government-issued photo ID (e.g., PhilSys National ID, Passport, Driver's License, Voter's ID) to verify your identity.  
* **Community Tax Certificate (Cedula):** An updated Cedula for the current calendar year.  
* **Proof of Residency:** A document proving you reside in the barangay, such as a recent utility bill (water, electricity, or internet) under your name, or a lease contract if you are renting. *(Note: Most barangays require you to have been a resident for at least six months).*  
* **Application Form:** Provided for free at the Barangay Hall.  
* **Clearance Fee:** Prepare around ₱20.00 to ₱150.00 depending on your barangay's local ordinance and the purpose of the clearance (e.g., personal vs. business use).  
* *(Note: If you are a First Time Job Seeker under RA 11261, securing a Barangay Clearance is FREE, provided you present an Oath of Undertaking and proof of graduation/completion of studies).*

**The Process:**

1. **Step 1: Secure and Accomplish the Application Form**  
   Visit your local Barangay Hall during office hours (typically 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM). Approach the Barangay Secretary or the desk officer and request an application form for a Barangay Clearance or Barangay ID. Fill it out completely with accurate personal details and the specific purpose of your request (e.g., for employment, bank account opening, or ID application).  
2. **Step 2: Submit Documents and Blotter Verification**  
   Submit your accomplished form, valid ID, Cedula, and proof of billing to the desk officer. The Barangay Secretary will verify your documents and cross-reference your name with the local barangay blotter database to ensure you have no pending mediation cases, unresolved complaints, or criminal citations in the community.  
3. **Step 3: Pay the Application Fee**  
   Once your record is cleared, proceed to the Barangay Treasurer or the designated cashier window. Pay the assessed clearance fee and secure your Official Receipt (OR).  
4. **Step 4: Biometrics Capture and Signing**  
   Return to the releasing desk with your receipt. You will be asked to affix your signature and provide your thumbmark (usually the right thumb) on the printed clearance. If your barangay issues digitized IDs or clearances, they will capture a live digital photograph of you on the spot.  
5. **Step 5: Release of the Barangay Clearance / ID**  
   Wait a few moments for the document to be finalized. The clearance must be signed by the incumbent Barangay Captain (or an authorized representative) and stamped with the official dry seal of the barangay to be considered legally valid. Review your details for any errors before leaving.

**Estimated Timelines:**

* **Processing Time:** Obtaining a Barangay Clearance or ID is generally an over-the-counter transaction. It typically takes **15 to 30 minutes** for walk-in applicants, depending on the queue and the availability of the Barangay Captain to sign the document.  
* **Validity:** Most Barangay Clearances are strictly valid for **six (6) months** from the date of issuance.

# PSA Birth Certificate

PSA Birth Certificate 

### **Step-by-Step Guide: PSA Birth Certificate**

**Prerequisites**

Before requesting a PSA Birth Certificate, ensure you have the following prepared:

* **Requester's Information:** The full name (first, middle, last) of the person on the certificate, their date of birth, place of birth (city/municipality and province), and the full names of both parents (including the mother's maiden name).  
* **Valid ID:** One (1) valid government-issued ID of the requesting party. The requester must be the document owner, a parent, a legal guardian, or an authorized representative.  
* **Authorization (if applicable):** If requesting as a representative, you need an authorization letter from the document owner, a photocopy of the document owner's valid ID, and your own valid ID.  
* **Fees:** ₱365 per copy via online order (delivery included) or ₱155 per copy for walk-in at a PSA outlet or Serbilis Center.

**The Process**

You have two options to acquire the document: Online or Walk-in.

**Option A: Online via PSAHelpline.ph (Recommended)**

1. **Step 1: Place your order online.** Go to PSAHelpline.ph, click "Order Now" under Birth Certificate, and fill out the online order form with the complete details of the person and your delivery address.  
2. **Step 2: Select quantity.** Choose the number of copies you need and proceed to checkout.  
3. **Step 3: Pay the fee.** Choose a payment method and pay the ₱365 fee. Accepted methods include GCash, Maya, credit/debit card, BancNet over-the-counter bank payment, and over-the-counter outlets like 7-Eleven or Bayad Center.  
4. **Step 4: Secure tracking reference.** A reference or tracking number will be sent to your registered email or mobile number so you can monitor your order status.  
5. **Step 5: Receive your document.** Wait for the certificate to be delivered to your address via courier (LBC or similar) and sign the delivery receipt upon claiming.

**Option B: Walk-In at a PSA Outlet or Serbilis Center**

1. **Step 1: Go to a PSA Outlet.** Go to the nearest PSA Civil Registration System (CRS) outlet, SM Business Center, or Robinsons Business Center offering PSA services, and get a queue number upon arrival.  
2. **Step 2: Fill out the application.** Fill out the application form provided on-site with all the required details of the document owner.  
3. **Step 3: Submit documents.** Submit the accomplished form and present your valid ID at the designated processing window.  
4. **Step 4: Pay the fee.** Pay the ₱155 fee per copy at the cashier and receive a claim stub with your order reference number.  
5. **Step 5: Claim the certificate.** Wait for your number to be called for document release, which is typically done on the same day.

**Estimated Timelines**

* **Walk-In Processing Time:** Released on the same day. Expect to spend approximately 1 to 3 hours at the outlet, depending on the queue.  
* **Online Processing Time:** Allow 3 to 5 working days for processing. Delivery within Metro Manila takes an additional 3 to 5 working days, while provincial deliveries may take 5 to 10 working days. The total end-to-end time online can reach up to 2 to 3 weeks in worst-case scenarios.  
* *(Note: If the birth record cannot be found in the PSA database, you will need to coordinate with the Local Civil Registry (LCR) of the birthplace for late registration or document correction, which significantly extends the timeline)*.

# ACR/ICR

### **Step-by-Step Guide: ACR I-Card (formerly ACR/ICR)**

**Prerequisites:**

Before heading to the Bureau of Immigration (BI), ensure you have the following requirements ready:

* **Eligibility Check:** You must be a foreign national who has been granted a visa and intends to stay in the Philippines for more than fifty-nine (59) days (this includes permanent immigrants, students, workers, and long-staying tourists).  
* **Application Form:** A duly accomplished ACR I-Card Application Form (BI Form), which can be downloaded online or secured at the BI office.  
* **Valid Passport:** Bring the original passport, along with photocopies of the bio-page, the page bearing the valid visa implementation, and the latest admission stamp.  
* **Visa Documents / Board Order:** A certified true copy of the Bureau of Commissioners (BOC) Order granting your visa or visa extension. *(Note: Permanent immigrants may also need to present proof of their immigrant status or marriage to a Filipino citizen, depending on the visa category).*  
* **ID Photos:** Two (2) recent 2x2 inch colored photographs with a white background.  
* **Fees:** The standard ACR I-Card fee is **$50.00 USD**. In addition to this, prepare Philippine Pesos (PHP) for local administrative fees, which usually include an Express Lane Fee (₱500.00 \- ₱1,000.00), a Legal Research Fee (₱10.00), and an Alien Registration Fee (₱1,000.00 for new registrants).

**The Process:**

1. **Step 1: Accomplish the Application Form**  
   Download the ACR I-Card application form from the BI E-Services website or obtain it directly from the Public Information and Assistance Unit at the BI office. Fill it out completely and accurately. (Some visa categories allow you to pre-register and secure an appointment online).  
2. **Step 2: Submit Documents for Pre-Screening**  
   Proceed to the Bureau of Immigration (BI) Main Office in Intramuros, Manila, or any authorized BI District/Field Office. Submit your accomplished form and all documentary prerequisites to the Central Receiving Unit or the designated ACR I-Card window. An immigration officer will verify your visa status and check your documents for completeness.  
3. **Step 3: Secure the Order of Payment and Pay Fees**  
   Once your documents are cleared, the evaluator will issue an Order of Payment Slip (OPS). Proceed to the designated cashier, present the OPS, and pay the required fees (the USD card fee and the PHP administrative fees). Keep your Official Receipt as proof of payment.  
4. **Step 4: Biometric Data Capture**  
   After paying, proceed to the biometrics capturing station. You will have your live digital photograph taken, your fingerprints scanned (left and right index fingers), and your electronic signature recorded.  
5. **Step 5: Claim Your ACR I-Card**  
   You will be issued a claim stub indicating when your card will be ready. Return to the BI office on the specified date, present your claim stub and original passport for verification, and claim your microchip-embedded ACR I-Card.

**Estimated Timelines:**

* **Processing Time:** Standard processing typically takes between **2 to 4 weeks** depending on your location and visa type. If you pay the Express Lane fee, the card can often be released within **7 to 10 working days** (or even faster in highly modernized offices).  
* **Validity:** The validity of the ACR I-Card is tied to your visa. For permanent residents (Immigrants), the card is usually valid for five (5) years. For non-immigrants (like workers or students), it is valid for one (1) year or for the duration of the visa, whichever is shorter. *(Note: All ACR I-Card holders are legally mandated to file an Annual Report in person at the BI within the first 60 days of every calendar year).*
